1. VISA & ENTRY REQUIREMENTS (as of 2025)
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Vietnam Visa-Free Entry (updated 2023-2024 policy):
- Citizens of the United States, United Kingdom, France, Germany, Italy, Spain, Japan, South Korea, and several other countries enjoy VISA-FREE entry for up to 45 days.
- This applies to single entry. If you exit and re-enter, a new 45-day period begins.
- Your passport must be valid for at least 6 months from the date of entry.

E-Visa Option:
- Available for citizens of 80+ countries.
- Valid for 90 days, single or multiple entry.
- Apply online at https://evisa.xuatnhapcanh.gov.vn
- Processing time: approximately 3 working days.
- Cost: approximately $25 USD.

Visa on Arrival:
- Available but requires a pre-approved invitation letter from a Vietnamese travel agency.
- Processing at the airport can take 30-60 minutes.

Important: Visa policies can change. Always verify the latest requirements on the official Vietnam Immigration Department website before traveling.

2. CURRENCY & EXCHANGE RATES
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Vietnamese Dong (VND):
- As of 2025, approximately 1 USD = 25,800 VND (this rate fluctuates daily).
- Common denominations: 10,000 / 20,000 / 50,000 / 100,000 / 200,000 / 500,000 VND notes.
- The 500,000 VND note (~$19 USD) is the largest denomination.

Other common exchange rates (approximate, 2025):
- 1 EUR ≈ 28,000 VND
- 1 GBP ≈ 32,500 VND
- 1 JPY ≈ 170 VND
- 1 KRW ≈ 19 VND
- 1 AUD ≈ 16,800 VND

Where to Exchange Money:
- Banks (Vietcombank, BIDV, Agribank) offer the best official rates.
- Licensed exchange shops in tourist areas (especially in Hanoi Old Quarter, Ben Thanh Market area in HCMC).
- Avoid exchanging at hotels — rates are usually 2-3% worse.
- ATMs are widely available in cities. Most charge a fee of 22,000-55,000 VND per withdrawal.
- International cards (Visa, Mastercard) work at most ATMs. Check with your bank about foreign transaction fees.

For real-time rates, check: XE.com, Vietcombank website, or your banking app.

3. TRANSPORTATION
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Hanoi to Ha Long Bay:
- Shuttle bus / Limousine van: 3.5-4 hours, costs approximately 200,000-350,000 VND ($8-14 USD) one way. Popular operators: Grouptour, Eco Sapa Limousine.
- Private car/taxi: 3-3.5 hours, costs approximately 1,500,000-2,000,000 VND ($60-80 USD) one way.
- Tour packages (most popular): Full-day or overnight cruise tours from Hanoi, starting from $50-150 USD/person including transport, meals, and activities.
- Seaplane (Hai Au Aviation): 45 minutes, ~$250 USD one way. Scenic but expensive.
- Note: There is NO direct train to Ha Long Bay. The nearest train station is Hai Phong, then transfer by bus.

Hanoi to Sapa:
- Overnight sleeper train: 8 hours, from 500,000-800,000 VND ($20-31 USD).
- Limousine van: 5-6 hours, from 300,000-500,000 VND ($12-20 USD).

Hanoi to Hoi An / Da Nang:
- Domestic flight: 1.5 hours. Airlines: VietJet Air, Bamboo Airways, Vietnam Airlines. Price range: $30-100 USD depending on booking time.
- The Reunification Express train: 15-17 hours. Sleeper berth from 700,000-1,200,000 VND.
- Overnight sleeper bus: 17-18 hours, from 400,000-600,000 VND.

Ho Chi Minh City to Phu Quoc:
- Domestic flight: 1 hour. Price: $25-70 USD.
- Bus + ferry combo: 7-8 hours total (bus to Ha Tien/Rach Gia, then ferry). Budget option ~300,000 VND.

Domestic Flights in Vietnam:
- Main airlines: Vietnam Airlines (full service), VietJet Air (budget), Bamboo Airways (mid-range).
- Typical one-way prices for popular routes: $30-100 USD booked 2-4 weeks in advance.
- For best prices, check Google Flights, Skyscanner, or book directly on airline websites.
- Domestic flights are generally affordable and save significant travel time.

Within Cities:
- Grab (ride-hailing app, like Uber): Most popular and reliable option. Available for motorbike and car rides.
- Cyclo (pedicab): Tourist experience in Hanoi Old Quarter and Hoi An. Negotiate price before riding.
- Local bus: Very cheap (7,000-8,000 VND per trip) but confusing for tourists.
- Motorbike rental: 100,000-200,000 VND/day ($4-8 USD). International driving permit required.

4. REGIONAL ATTRACTIONS
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════

NORTHERN VIETNAM:
- Hanoi: The capital city. Visit Ho Chi Minh Mausoleum, Temple of Literature, Hoan Kiem Lake, Old Quarter with its 36 streets. Famous for egg coffee (cà phê trứng) and phở.
- Ha Long Bay: UNESCO World Heritage Site. Thousands of limestone karsts rising from emerald waters. Best experienced on overnight cruise boats.
- Sapa: Mountain town near Chinese border. Famous for terraced rice fields, ethnic minority villages (Hmong, Dao), and trekking. Best season: September-October (golden rice season).
- Ninh Binh: "Ha Long Bay on land". Visit Trang An, Tam Coc (boat rides through limestone caves and rice paddies), and Bai Dinh Pagoda.
- Mu Cang Chai: Remote mountain district in Yen Bai province. Famous for spectacular terraced rice fields. Best time to visit: late September to early October when the rice turns golden before harvest. Accessible from Hanoi by bus/motorbike (about 6 hours). Less touristy than Sapa.
- Ha Giang: Northernmost province with dramatic mountain passes. The Ha Giang Loop is one of the most scenic motorcycle routes in Southeast Asia.

CENTRAL VIETNAM:
- Hoi An: UNESCO Ancient Town. Lantern-lit streets, tailor shops, Japanese Covered Bridge. Best explored on foot or by bicycle. Famous for Cao Lau, Mi Quang, and White Rose dumplings.
- Da Nang: Modern coastal city. Visit Marble Mountains, Dragon Bridge, Ba Na Hills (Golden Bridge). Great beaches (My Khe Beach).
- Hue: Former imperial capital. Visit the Imperial Citadel, Thien Mu Pagoda, Royal Tombs. Famous for Bun Bo Hue (spicy beef noodle soup).
- Phong Nha-Ke Bang National Park: World's largest caves including Son Doong Cave. Adventure trekking and caving options.
- Quy Nhon: Emerging beach destination, less crowded than Da Nang. Beautiful coastline and Cham ruins.

SOUTHERN VIETNAM:
- Ho Chi Minh City (Saigon): Vietnam's largest city. Visit War Remnants Museum, Notre-Dame Cathedral, Ben Thanh Market, Cu Chi Tunnels. Vibrant street food and nightlife scene.
- Mekong Delta: "Rice bowl of Vietnam". Floating markets (Cai Rang, Cai Be), coconut candy villages, boat rides through mangrove forests.
- Phu Quoc Island: Tropical island with pristine beaches. Best for beach relaxation, snorkeling, night markets. Vietnam's largest island.
- Con Dao Islands: Remote and less developed. Beautiful beaches, excellent diving, historical significance (former political prison).
- Da Lat: Highland city at 1,500m elevation. Cool climate, French colonial architecture, flower gardens. Known as the "City of Eternal Spring".

5. FOOD & CUISINE BY REGION
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════

MUST-TRY DISHES:
- Pho (Phở): Vietnam's iconic noodle soup. Hanoi-style (phở Bắc) is cleaner and more subtle. Saigon-style (phở Nam) has more herbs and bean sprouts.
- Banh Mi (Bánh Mì): Vietnamese baguette sandwich with pate, cold cuts, pickled vegetables, cilantro, and chili. The world's best street sandwich.
- Bun Cha: Grilled pork with rice noodles and dipping sauce. A Hanoi specialty (famously eaten by Obama and Bourdain at Bun Cha Huong Lien).
- Bun Bo Hue: Spicy beef noodle soup from Hue. Thicker noodles, lemongrass-spiced broth, often with pork blood cake.
- Com Tam (Cơm Tấm): Broken rice with grilled pork chop, egg, and pickled vegetables. A Saigon street food staple.
- Banh Xeo (Bánh Xèo): Crispy Vietnamese crepe filled with shrimp, pork, and bean sprouts.
- Ca Phe Sua Da: Vietnamese iced coffee with condensed milk. Strong, sweet, and addictive.

HOI AN SPECIALTIES (must-try when visiting):
- Cao Lau (cao lầu): Thick rice noodles with pork, greens, and crispy croutons in a small amount of broth. Unique to Hoi An — the noodles are traditionally made with water from a specific local well.
- Mi Quang (mì Quảng): Turmeric-colored wide rice noodles with pork/shrimp, peanuts, and herbs. Served with a small amount of rich broth.
- White Rose (Bánh Bao Bánh Vạc): Translucent shrimp dumplings shaped like roses. Made by only one family in Hoi An.
- Banh Mi Phuong (Bánh Mì Phượng): One of the most famous banh mi stalls in Vietnam. Featured by Anthony Bourdain.
- Com Ga Pho Hoi (Cơm Gà Phố Hội): Hoi An chicken rice — turmeric-infused rice with shredded chicken, herbs, and special sauce.

6. DIETARY NEEDS & FOOD SAFETY
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════

VEGETARIAN / VEGAN TRAVELERS:
- Vietnam has a strong Buddhist vegetarian tradition called "Cơm Chay" (chay = vegetarian).
- Many locals eat vegetarian on the 1st and 15th of each lunar month.
- Look for restaurants with signs saying "Cơm Chay" or "Quán Chay" — these serve entirely vegetarian menus.
- Useful Vietnamese phrases:
  - "Tôi ăn chay" = I am vegetarian
  - "Không có thịt" = No meat
  - "Không có cá" = No fish
  - "Tôi không ăn thịt và cá" = I don't eat meat and fish
- WARNING: Fish sauce (nước mắm) is used in almost ALL Vietnamese dishes, even vegetable dishes. Specifically request "Không nước mắm" (no fish sauce) when ordering. Many "Cơm Chay" restaurants use soy-based alternatives.
- Tofu (đậu phụ/đậu hũ), mushrooms, and mock-meat dishes are widely available at chay restaurants.

HALAL FOOD:
- Halal options are available in major cities, especially Ho Chi Minh City and Hanoi.
- In HCMC:
  - Near Ben Thanh Market (District 1): Several halal restaurants and food stalls.
  - Cham community areas in District 8 and parts of District 1.
  - Specific restaurants: Al-Jazeera, Saigon I Love Halal, Mumbai Indian Restaurant.
- In Hanoi: Limited but growing. Check Halal certification at restaurants near Hoan Kiem Lake area.
- Outside major cities, halal options are very limited. Consider cooking or eating at international hotels.
- Download the "HalalTrip" or "Zabihah" app for finding halal restaurants.
- Be aware that pork is the most common meat in Vietnamese cuisine.

FOOD ALLERGIES:
- Peanuts (Đậu phộng/Lạc): Very commonly used in Vietnamese cuisine — in sauces, toppings, and stir-fries. Found in Bun Cha dipping sauce, Goi Cuon sauce, Mi Quang, many salads. Always ask about peanuts.
  Useful phrase: "Tôi bị dị ứng với đậu phộng" (I am allergic to peanuts)
- Shellfish/Seafood (Hải sản): Common in coastal areas. Shrimp paste (mắm tôm) is used in some dishes.
  Useful phrase: "Tôi bị dị ứng với hải sản" (I am allergic to seafood)
- Fish (Cá): Fish sauce is ubiquitous. If you have a fish allergy, you must specifically ask for no fish sauce as well.
  Useful phrase: "Tôi bị dị ứng với cá, không nước mắm" (I am allergic to fish, no fish sauce)
- Gluten: Vietnamese cuisine is relatively gluten-friendly as rice is the staple. However, soy sauce (contains wheat) and some noodles may contain gluten.
  Useful phrase: "Tôi không ăn được gluten" (I cannot eat gluten)
- GENERAL TIP: Write your allergy in Vietnamese on a card or your phone to show restaurant staff. This is much more effective than saying it verbally, as many staff may not understand English.
- Despite allergy concerns, Vietnam has MANY safe food options. Rice dishes, pho (without specific allergens), fruit, and simple grilled meats are generally safe choices when communicated properly.

7. WEATHER & BEST TIME TO VISIT
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Vietnam has diverse climate zones:

NORTH (Hanoi, Ha Long, Sapa):
- Best time: October-December (cool, dry, clear skies) and March-April (spring, warm).
- Summer (June-August): Hot and humid, 30-35°C, occasional heavy rain.
- Winter (December-February): Cool, sometimes cold in mountains (Sapa can drop to 0°C).

CENTRAL (Hue, Da Nang, Hoi An):
- Best time: February-May (dry, warm, 25-30°C).
- Rainy season: September-November (typhoon season, heavy flooding possible in Hoi An).
- Avoid October-November if possible due to typhoon risk.

SOUTH (HCMC, Mekong Delta, Phu Quoc):
- Dry season: December-April (best time, sunny, 28-35°C).
- Rainy season: May-November (afternoon thunderstorms, usually brief).
- Temperature is consistently warm/hot year-round.

Note: I cannot provide real-time weather data or specific forecasts. For current weather, check AccuWeather (accuweather.com), Weather.com, or Windy (windy.com).

8. BUDGET TRAVEL GUIDE
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Budget Estimates (per person, per day):
- Budget traveler: $25-40 USD/day (hostels, street food, local transport)
- Mid-range traveler: $50-80 USD/day (3-star hotels, restaurants, mix of transport)
- Comfort traveler: $100-200+ USD/day (4-5 star hotels, tours, premium experiences)

Sample 7-Day Budget Itinerary (~$500 total):

Day 1-2: Hanoi (~$50-60)
- Accommodation: Hostel in Old Quarter ($8-12/night)
- Food: Street food pho ($1.50), banh mi ($1), egg coffee ($2)
- Activities: Walking tour of Old Quarter (free), Temple of Literature ($1.50), Hoan Kiem Lake (free)

Day 3: Hanoi → Ha Long Bay (~$80-100)
- Budget overnight cruise: $60-80/person (includes transport, meals, kayaking)

Day 4: Ha Long Bay → Hanoi → fly to Da Nang (~$60-80)
- Budget flight: VietJet Air $30-50 one-way
- Evening: Walk along Han River, Dragon Bridge light show (free, weekends)

Day 5-6: Hoi An (~$50-60)
- Accommodation: Homestay $10-15/night
- Bicycle rental: $1-2/day
- Food: Cao Lau ($1.50), Mi Quang ($1.50), Banh Mi Phuong ($1.50)
- Activities: Ancient Town walking (free, ticket for specific sites $5), beach (free)

Day 7: Da Nang → Ho Chi Minh City → explore (~$50-60)
- Budget flight: $30-50
- Ben Thanh Market, War Remnants Museum ($2), street food tour

TOTAL: ~$400-500 for a full 7-day experience.

9. PRACTICAL TIPS
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════

- SIM Card: Buy a local SIM at the airport (Viettel, Mobifone). ~$5-10 for 30 days with data.
- Bargaining: Expected at markets and street vendors. Start at 50-60% of asking price.
- Tipping: Not required but appreciated. Round up taxi fares, tip $1-2 at restaurants for good service.
- Safety: Vietnam is generally very safe for tourists. Be aware of petty theft (bag snatching from motorbikes) in HCMC. Keep valuables secure.
- Water: Do NOT drink tap water. Bottled water is cheap (5,000-10,000 VND). Ice in tourist areas is usually safe (factory-made).
- Electricity: 220V, Type A/C/G plugs. Bring a universal adapter.
- Internet: Free WiFi is available almost everywhere — cafes, hotels, restaurants.
